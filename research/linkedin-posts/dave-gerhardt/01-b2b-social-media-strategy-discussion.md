## Public Content Summary

This source is an Exit Five podcast episode focused directly on B2B social media strategy. The public episode information frames the discussion around organic marketing, LinkedIn content, executive content, video, ROI, and personal versus company pages. The source addresses the practical problem that B2B teams often know they should use LinkedIn and social media, but struggle to decide who should post, what format to use, how to measure impact, and how to balance individual voices against corporate channels.

The source presents social strategy as more than a publishing calendar. The public description indicates that effective B2B social work includes executive visibility, thoughtful use of video, and a clear view of ROI. It also highlights a common strategic tension: personal pages often carry stronger reach and trust, while company pages still matter for brand presence. - Personal pages and company pages are separate channels with different strategic roles.
- ROI is a relevant question for organic social, not only paid campaigns.

## Practical Takeaways

- Evaluate whether key executives should publish from personal profiles as part of the social strategy.
- Treat company page content and personal profile content as related but different motions.
- Include video in the LinkedIn/social planning conversation when relevant.
- Define what ROI means before judging organic social performance.
