## Public Content Summary

This source is a public episode summary from Positioning with April Dunford about post-positioning testing and execution. The summary says April prefers testing positioning through live sales conversations. The problem addressed is that teams may complete a positioning exercise but still need to validate whether the new positioning works in real buyer-facing situations.

The source teaches that positioning is not finished when the workshop or document is done. It must be tested through conversations where prospects can react, ask questions, and reveal confusion. For LinkedIn strategy, this matters because public content should not be built only from internal assumptions. If a message fails in sales conversations, it may also fail in organic thought leadership. The public source does not provide a transcript, so the note is limited to the summary-level ideas visible on the page.

## Key Ideas

- Positioning should be tested after it is created.
- Live sales conversations are identified as a preferred testing environment.
- Execution matters after the positioning work is complete.
