## Public Content Summary

This source is a Positioning podcast episode summary about market categories. The public summary says companies overcomplicate categories and can create confusion by starting with the wrong category. The problem addressed is category confusion: when a company frames itself in the wrong market context, buyers may misunderstand what it does or compare it against the wrong alternatives.

The source teaches that category choices are foundational to positioning. For LinkedIn content, category clarity matters because public posts often educate the market about what problem space the company belongs in. If the category is confusing, thought leadership and founder-led content can amplify that confusion. The accessible source is a summary rather than a transcript, so it does not provide a step-by-step category selection method.

## Key Ideas

- Companies can overcomplicate market categories.
- Starting with the wrong category can create buyer confusion.
- Category clarity is part of positioning.
- Public content can either clarify or worsen category understanding.
- LinkedIn strategy should be aligned with the chosen market category.
