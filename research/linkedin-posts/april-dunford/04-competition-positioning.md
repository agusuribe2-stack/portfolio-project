## Public Content Summary

This source is a Positioning podcast episode summary about competition in a positioning exercise. The public summary says competitive alternatives are the right place to start a positioning exercise. The problem addressed is that companies may define themselves internally without first understanding what buyers would otherwise choose.

The source teaches that positioning should be anchored in buyer alternatives. For LinkedIn content, this matters because strong posts often contrast old ways and new ways, or explain why a common alternative creates problems. The source supports competitive context as a foundation for market education. The public summary does not provide the full exercise, but the available concept is specific and useful.

This source matters because many strong B2B SaaS LinkedIn posts are built around contrast.
